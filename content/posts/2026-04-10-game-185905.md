In a shocking revelation, employees of Gunzilla Games, the blockchain game studio co-founded by renowned director Neill Blomkamp, have alleged that they have not been paid for several months. This situation raises significant concerns about the treatment of artists and developers in the gaming industry, particularly within new and emerging companies that leverage blockchain technology.

Former animator Paul Creamer took to LinkedIn to voice his frustrations, claiming he hasnt received his salary since October 2025. His experience is echoed by other staff members who report similar issues, highlighting a troubling pattern of unpaid wages that could undermine the morale and financial stability of creative professionals in the tech and gaming sectors. The allegations suggest a broader issue of accountability in the industry, where the passion of artists is often exploited under the guise of startup challenges.

As the gaming landscape continues to evolve with blockchain integration, its crucial for industry leaders and stakeholders to address these concerns proactively. Will this incident serve as a wake-up call for better practices in employee compensation and transparency in the gaming industry? Only time will tell.
