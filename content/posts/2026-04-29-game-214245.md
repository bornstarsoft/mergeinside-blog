In a significant move to combat piracy, 2K Games and Denuvo have reportedly introduced mandatory 14-day online checks for certain titles, including NBA 2K26 and Marvels Midnight Suns. This decision comes on the heels of hackers claiming to have fully cracked Denuvos DRM protections, which has raised concerns about the impact of such software on game performance and accessibility.

The new system requires players to connect online every two weeks to renew their authorization token, effectively locking out those who play offline for extended periods. This could pose challenges for gamers who prefer to play on portable devices like the Steam Deck, as they may find themselves unable to access their games without a stable internet connection.

As the gaming industry continues to grapple with the balance between protecting intellectual property and ensuring a seamless user experience, this development raises questions about the future of DRM in gaming. Will players accept these new restrictions, or will they push back against what they see as an infringement on their rights? Only time will tell.
