NHN is making a significant push to revitalize its gaming business by launching two new titles, 'Abyssdia' and 'Oshi no Ko: Puzzle Star', simultaneously. While the initial market response has been promising, with both games achieving top rankings in their respective app stores, the real challenge lies in converting this early interest into sustainable revenue. The company's CEO has set ambitious revenue growth targets, but the gaming sector's growth has lagged behind other divisions, raising concerns among investors.

The gaming industry is notoriously volatile, and NHN's reliance on external IPs for its new titles could complicate its profitability trajectory. Despite the excitement surrounding these launches, analysts remain cautious, noting that past experiences with similar strategies have not always yielded the expected financial returns. As NHN navigates these challenges, the potential impact of deregulation in the web board game sector may provide a silver lining, offering new revenue opportunities.

As we watch NHN's strategy unfold, it raises an important question: Can the company successfully transform initial buzz into lasting success in a competitive gaming landscape?
